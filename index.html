Chasing European Sunsets

Join the Journey!

Welcome to my travel journal! I'm Danny, and this is the complete diary of my recent adventure across Europe. Over 30 days, I backpacked through **six amazing countries**, chasing history, spectacular views, and the best local food. From the icy fjords of **Norway** to the ancient ruins of **Greece**, and the warm thermal baths of **Hungary**, every stop was a new discovery. Grab a snack and follow the route!

NORWAY • September 28, 2025

Oslo's Modern Marvels and Sculptural Giants

From gliding on the Opera House roof to walking through Vigeland's powerful park, Oslo is a city of quiet, beautiful contrasts.

1. The White Marble Wonder: Oslo Opera House

The Oslo Opera House is an architectural masterpiece that seems to rise directly from the Oslofjord. The most incredible part of this building is its sloping roof, which acts as a public piazza. We spent a wonderful hour just walking right up to the top, taking in the panoramic views of the city, the harbor, and the surrounding islands. The way the white marble reflects the light is simply stunning—it feels more like an ice floe than a building.

2. Vigeland Sculpture Park: The Circle of Life in Bronze

Gustav Vigeland's permanent installation in Frogner Park is unlike anything else. It's a vast, open-air gallery dedicated entirely to the human form and the cycle of life. Every sculpture, from the angry babies to the powerful **"Monolith,"** tells a story of human experience—joy, sorrow, frustration, and love. We covered miles just studying the details of the bronze figures.

Caption: The powerful Monolith towering over the Vigeland Sculpture Park.

3. Skiing History & Oslo After Dark

Even in the early stages of autumn/winter, the **Holmenkollen Ski Jump** is an impressive sight. The stadium is massive, and seeing the massive slope built for ski jumping world cups gives you a great sense of Norway's deep connection to winter sports. The early sunsets meant we got to experience Oslo's beauty in the evening. The city lights twinkling across the hills, often dusted with the first snow, made for some truly cozy views.

Holmenkollen Ski Jump, an impressive sight.

Peaceful, glowing view of Oslo after the first snowfall.

DENMARK • October 3, 2025

Copenhagen Charm: Canals, Cobblestones, and Fairytale Thrills

A trip through Denmark's capital, from the iconic harbor to historic towers and Europe's oldest amusement park.

1. Colorful Canals of Nyhavn

**Nyhavn** is exactly as picture-perfect as the postcards make it out to be! The line of brightly colored 17th-century townhouses reflecting in the canal is simply breathtaking. We took a canal tour from here, which was the perfect way to see the city and learn about Hans Christian Andersen, who lived in several of these charming houses.

2. The Heart of Danish Democracy

We managed to take a tour of the **Christiansborg Palace**, which houses the Danish Parliament (Folketinget). It was fascinating to see the inner workings of the government in such a historic, yet active, setting. The ornate wood paneling and curved seating arrangement were impressive.

Caption: Inside the Folketinget, the Danish Parliament.

3. A Night at Tivoli Gardens

**Tivoli** is more than just an amusement park; it's a piece of living history and absolute pure joy. We spent the evening there, and the lights, the gardens, and the classic rides create a truly magical atmosphere. It's famous for inspiring Walt Disney!

Caption: The dazzling lights of Tivoli Gardens make for a perfect evening.

HUNGARY • October 10, 2025

Budapest Bliss: Castles, Views, and Thermal Warmth

Exploring the incredible Hungarian capital, split by the Danube and known for its majestic buildings and healing waters.

1. Royalty and Fairytales: Buda Castle and Fisherman's Bastion

The Buda side of the city offers incredible historic sights and the best views. The real showstopper is the **Fisherman's Bastion**. It looks like something straight out of a Disney movie, with its seven white stone towers. The view of Pest bathed in golden light was unforgettable! It was even dusted with snow when we visited!

Caption: Taking in the snowy cityscape from the Buda Castle area (with some new friends!).

2. The Majestic Nightline: Chain Bridge and Parliament

The **Hungarian Parliament Building** on the Pest side is simply awe-inspiring. It's the largest building in Hungary, built in a stunning Neo-Gothic style. Seeing it lit up at night from across the Danube, framed by the beautiful **Chain Bridge**, is a classic Budapest experience that you simply cannot miss.

Caption: The Chain Bridge and St. Stephen's Basilica glowing brightly over the Danube River.

3. Thermal Warmth: A Budapest Essential

The essential Budapest experience is visiting a **Thermal Bath**. We chose the famous **Széchenyi Baths**. It was an amazing way to relax after days of walking, especially when the evening air was cold. The contrast of the hot, steamy water against the night sky is unforgettable!

Caption: Keeping warm in the historic Széchenyi Thermal Baths at night.

GREECE • October 18, 2025

Athens: Walking Among Gods and Heroes

Standing on the Acropolis, you realize you're walking through the birthplace of democracy and philosophy. A truly humbling experience.

1. High City: The Acropolis, Parthenon, and Odeon

The **Acropolis of Athens** is the heart of the ancient world. Climbing the hill and seeing the **Parthenon** up close is breathtaking. Just below, we saw the **Herod Atticus Odeon**, a restored stone theater still used for performances today.

Standing proudly in front of the majestic Parthenon.

Overlooking the ancient Odeon of Herod Atticus.

2. Inside the Museum: Preserving Ancient Stories

The modern **Acropolis Museum** is a must-visit. It houses every major object found on the Acropolis rock. The most fascinating floor is the top one, laid out exactly like the Parthenon itself.

3. Heights and the Agora: Lycabettus and Hephaestus

For the best perspective, we headed up **Mount Lycabettus**. We also explored the ancient Agora and visited the incredibly well-preserved **Temple of Hephaestus**, one of the most intact ancient Greek temples in the world.

Caption: The majestic Panathenaic Stadium, host of the first modern Olympics.

NETHERLANDS • October 25, 2025

Amsterdam Adventure: Gables, Bicycles, and Golden Age Art

From the bustling central hub to tranquil canals, exploring the stunning architecture and creative heart of the Dutch capital.

1. Grand Gateway: Amsterdam Centraal Station

Arriving at **Amsterdam Centraal Station** is an experience in itself! It looks more like a royal palace than a train station. Stepping outside, you’re greeted by hundreds of bicycles—the main way to get around Amsterdam!

Caption: A cute souvenir capturing the city's love for bicycles.

2. The Beautiful Canal Ring and Gabled Houses

The **Canal Ring** is a UNESCO World Heritage site. The tall, narrow houses with their iconic gabled roofs lean slightly, a quirky result of building on soft ground centuries ago.

Caption: The magical view of an Amsterdam canal at night.

3. A Day of Art and History at Museumplein

For a dose of culture, the **Museumplein** (Museum Square) is the place to be. It’s surrounded by museums like the **Rijksmuseum** and the **Van Gogh Museum**.

Caption: The unique atmosphere of Amsterdam's streets after dark.

BELGIUM • October 28, 2025

Brussels: The Grand Place, Comic Strips, and Delicious Eats

From golden guildhalls to deep-fried perfection, exploring the charming, cosmopolitan, and delicious heart of Belgium.

1. A Fairytale Square: Grand Place's Light Show

The **Grand Place** is one of the most stunning squares in the world. Surrounded by ornate Baroque guildhalls decorated with gold, it’s an architectural masterpiece during the day. But at night, the square comes alive with an incredible sound and light show that bathes the Town Hall in rotating, brilliant colors. It's a truly spectacular way to end a day of sightseeing.

Caption: The Brussels Town Hall during the captivating evening light show.

2. The Taste of Belgium: Frites and Waffles

You absolutely cannot visit Belgium without trying the food! The famous **Belgian Waffle** is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, and of course, topped with chocolate and nuts. We also grabbed a classic cone of **Frites** (Belgian Fries), which are double-fried for extra crunch and served with an incredible array of dipping sauces. Pure culinary heaven!

Sampling the famous Belgian waffle on the streets of Brussels.

So many toppings, so little time! Waffle paradise.

Enjoying the perfectly double-fried frites—a true Belgian staple.

3. Comic Strips, Beer, and the Atomium

We also spent time admiring Brussels' other charms, including the famous comic strip murals found all over the city (Tintin and the Smurfs originate here!). While we didn't visit **Mini-Europe**, we did see the futuristic **Atomium** structure from a distance, which was a nice contrast to the historic Grand Place.
